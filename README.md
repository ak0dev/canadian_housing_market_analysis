# canadian_housing_market_analysis

## Overview of Project
Analysis of Canadian housing price trends across 10 major cities using Python, SQL, and Statistics Canada data. The project investigates which markets experienced the greatest growth, quantifies the pandemic-era price surge by city, and measures the impact of the Bank of Canada's 2022 rate hike on regional markets. 

## Key findings
1. Market growth was led by Montreal with 54.16% growth between 2015-2024, followed by Winnipeg at 44.07%, both outpacing Toronto’s housing market growth at 25.7%. 
2. Ontario lost a net 41,929 residents to other procinces in 2022/2023, directly fuelling the price growth in receiving housing markets like Calgary and Winnipeg. 
3. The March 2022 rate hikes redistributed demand rather than eliminating it, Calgary saw 27.43% growth post-hike while Toronto grew just 7.18%, the smallest of any market analyzed.

## Tools & Data
- Languages: Python, SQL
- Libraries: Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn, SQLite3
- Data: Statistics Canada - New housing price index, monthly (Table: 18-10-0205-01)
